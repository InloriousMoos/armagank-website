Research

Experience design of personal
health technologies

I work on discovering ways to better understand people's experiences with digital health data — and developing tools and research to make those developments more tangible and long-lasting.

Research focus

We live in a data-driven world, which triggers the way research in health technology is carried out. Digital health technologies become increasingly data-driven, often neglecting how people actually experience their health data. As an expert in experience design, I work on discovering ways to better understand people's experiences with digital health data.

Currently, I am interested in the roles of interaction design in health and wellbeing, especially in self-tracking of physical activity and sports. My work combines physiology and psychology with interaction design in understanding and designing meaningful data interactions for engagement in physical activity and sports.

Key research questions I pursue:

  • What are the current user practices of meaningful data interactions in personal health technologies?
  • How can we transform this knowledge to novel personal health technologies?
  • What should be the role of designers in this transformation?
